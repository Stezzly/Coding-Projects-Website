Welcome to Blender Project 2 - The Coffee Mug

This project guides you through the steps to create a 3D model of a coffee mug using Blender. Perfect for beginners looking to get started with 3D modeling!

Hardware Setup

In this section, we'll outline all the hardware components required for the project. This includes:

  • A computer with at least 8 GB of RAM to ensure smooth operation.
  • A graphics card with at least 2 GB of VRAM (4 GB or more recommended for optimal performance).
  • A mouse with a scroll wheel, which is very useful for navigating in 3D space.

Software Requirements

Here, we'll detail all the software needed for the project, including:

  • Blender: Latest version available for free from the official Blender Foundation website.
  • Graphics driver: Updated graphics driver compatible with your graphics card to handle rendering efficiently.
  • Optional: Add-ons for Blender such as Node Wrangler for advanced material work, though not required for this beginner project.

Knowledge Prerequisites

To successfully complete this project, it's recommended to have a basic understanding of the following:

  • Basic computer skills: Comfort with operating systems (Windows, macOS, or Linux).
  • Understanding of 3D space and coordinates: Familiarity with how 3D space operates, including axes of x, y, and z.
  • Some familiarity with graphic design principles, such as shading, texture, and color basics.

  1. Open Blender and start a new project.
  2. Delete the default cube by selecting it (right-click or left-click depending on your selection preference) and pressing Delete on your keyboard.

  1. Press Shift + A to open the Add menu.
  2. Select Mesh then Cylinder.
  3. Set Vertices to 32, Radius to 1, Depth to 3, and Cap Fill Type to Ngon.

  1. Switch to Edit Mode by pressing Tab.
  2. With the entire cylinder selected, press S then Shift + Z and scale it on the X and Y axes only to make it wider.
  3. To create the thickness of the mug walls, press I to inset the top face slightly.
  4. Press E then Z to extrude the inset face down to create the inner wall of the mug, almost to the bottom of the cylinder.

  1. Back in Object Mode (Tab to toggle), add another cylinder with Vertices: 12, Radius: 0.3, Depth: 3.
  2. Rotate the new cylinder 90 degrees on the X-axis (R, X, 90, Enter).
  3. Move it to the side of the mug and modify it into an oval shape by scaling it (S, then Y).
  4. Position the oval where you'd like the handle to be.
  5. Go to Edit Mode, select half of the vertices of the oval, and move them to form a more handle-like shape.
  6. Attach the ends of the handle to the mug using Ctrl+J to join them into a single object, then manually merge the vertices using the Bridge tool or by merging vertices.

  1. Go to the Material Properties tab (red sphere icon).
  2. Click New to create a new material.
  3. Set the Base Color to your preference.
  4. Adjust other settings like Specular and Roughness to achieve the ceramic look.

  1. Position the camera to frame your mug appropriately. Select the camera, use G to move it, and R to rotate.
  2. Add a light source from Shift + A > Light > Point or Area. Position it to illuminate the mug effectively.
  3. Adjust light properties for intensity and color in the Light Properties tab.

  1. Go to the Render Properties panel.
  2. Choose your Render Engine (Eevee for speed, Cycles for quality).
  3. Press F12 to render your image.

  1. After rendering, go to Image > Save As in the render window to save your rendered image.
