Welcome to Blender!

This will be a straightforward introduction for those who are new to 3D modeling, animation, and rendering.
This section is focused on Blender, a powerful, versatile, and free 3D creation suite.
It will give you all the basics to get Blender set up on your computer.

In simple terms, we need a system capable of handling Blender's requirements, along with familiarizing yourself with its interface and primary functions. By the end of this introduction, you will have installed Blender and created your first simple 3D model.

Hardware Setup

In this section, we'll outline all the hardware components required for using Blender effectively. This includes:

  • Processor: Multi-core CPU (64-bit), 8 threads or more recommended.
  • Memory: Minimum 8 GB RAM, 16 GB or more recommended for heavy tasks.
  • Graphics Card: OpenGL 3.3 compatible with at least 2 GB RAM.
  • Storage: SSD with at least 20 GB of free space.

Software Requirements

Here, we'll detail all the software needed for using Blender:

  • Operating System of your choice (Windows 10 or later, macOS 10.13 High Sierra or later, or a compatible Linux distribution).
  • Blender: Download the latest Blender release from the official Blender website.

Knowledge Prerequisites

To successfully use Blender, it's recommended to have a basic understanding of the following:

  • 3D Modeling Concepts: Familiarity with basic 3D modeling terms and techniques.
  • Computer Graphics: Understanding basic concepts like lighting, texturing, and rendering.
